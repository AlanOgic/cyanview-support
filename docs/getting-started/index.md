# Getting started with Cyanview

Welcome to the Cyanview documentation. Whether you are setting up your first RCP or integrating a complex multi-camera REMI workflow, you will find everything you need here.

## Choose your setup path

### PTZ or IP cameras

Control Panasonic PTZ, Sony FX9, Canon C300, or any IP-connected camera directly from your RCP — no additional hardware needed.

1. [Connect your IP camera](/docs/integrations/generic/ip-camera)
2. [Set up tally](/docs/guides/tally/tally)
3. [Add a PTZ joystick](/docs/integrations/accessories/anxinshi-ptz-usb-joystick) or [Stream Deck](/docs/integrations/accessories/elgato-stream-deck)

### Serial or mini cameras

Connect Sony 8-pin, LANC, or VISCA serial cameras through a [CI0](/docs/products/ci0) or [RIO](/docs/products/rio).

1. [Connect your serial camera](/docs/integrations/generic/serial-camera)
2. [Configure tally](/docs/guides/tally/tally)

### Cinema cameras with lens control

For full camera and lens control with autonomous operation, use a [RIO](/docs/products/rio).

1. Configure the RIO and your camera ([serial](/docs/integrations/generic/serial-camera) or [IP](/docs/integrations/generic/ip-camera))
2. Set up your lens ([B4](/docs/integrations/lenses/b4-lens) or [Cine/Tilta](/docs/integrations/lenses/tilta))
3. [Import the camera into your RCP via REMI](/docs/guides/networking/remi)

### Camcorders with video processing

Use the [VP4](/docs/products/vp4) for multi-camera color correction and CCU control.

1. [VP4 manual](/docs/reference/manuals/vp4-manual)
2. [Tally configuration](/docs/guides/tally/tally)
